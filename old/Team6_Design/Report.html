Short Paragraph

In crafting our UML class diagram, our primary objective was to maintain low coupling and high cohesion. We maintained low coupling by employing relationships such as association, inheritance, and composition to facilitate data sharing, a practice known as data coupling. For example, we have separate classes for the 'Manager', 'Provider', and 'Operator' joined into the class employee. This allowed us to share necessary data of those classes among other classes while still minimizing other internal details. For instance, modules like 'addMember' and 'removeMember' exemplify high cohesion by executing specific actions related to member or provider editing. Each module focuses on performing precisely one action, thereby enhancing reusability and maintainability. By maximizing relationships within the diagram, we ensured ease of editing, fostering a flexible and easily maintainable system. Our approach emphasizes the importance of designing systems that minimize interdependencies between components while maximizing the coherence and clarity of individual modules. This approach ensures that our system is easy to maintain over time.
